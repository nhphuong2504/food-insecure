# EE 5290 Final Project: Predictive Targeting and Segmentation of Food-Insecure Households

A library-first machine-learning analysis of household food insecurity built on the
provided FoodAPS-derived public-use dataset. The project is structured as two
parallel tracks unified by a single applied framing: **how can a Midwest food
assistance organization with limited capacity decide which households to
prioritize, and what kinds of households exist among the food-insecure?**

- **Track 1 - Prediction.** Compare logistic regression, RBF-kernel SVM, and
  random forest classifiers on a binary food-insecurity outcome, with explicit
  treatment of class imbalance (none / class-weight balanced / threshold tuning).
- **Track 2 - Segmentation.** Use PCA followed by K-Means on the food-insecure
  subset to discover household archetypes whose profiles can inform targeting.

## Reproduction

Run notebooks in order:

1. `notebooks/01_eda_and_cleaning.ipynb` - cleans sentinel codes, encodes
   features, performs the 80/20 stratified split, and saves the cleaned
   matrices to `artifacts/`.
2. `notebooks/02_prediction_track.ipynb` - fits the 3 classifiers under 3
   imbalance strategies, runs grid search via 5-fold stratified CV on the
   training fold, and reports test-set metrics.
3. `notebooks/03_segmentation_track.ipynb` - fits PCA, sweeps K for K-Means,
   produces stability and profile diagnostics on the food-insecure subset.
4. `notebooks/04_synthesis_figures.ipynb` - regenerates the figures that
   appear in the final report at publication quality.

A single random seed (`RANDOM_STATE = 42`) is used throughout for
reproducibility.

## Scope and non-goals

This is a solo academic project completed under a tight timeline. To keep
the scope realistic and the analysis honest:

- We make no causal claims; findings are framed as associations and as
  inputs to risk targeting.
- We do not implement algorithms from scratch; all methods are used through
  scikit-learn.
- We do not use deep learning, external data, or survey weights in modeling
  (the latter is acknowledged as a limitation).
